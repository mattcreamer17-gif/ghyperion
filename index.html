A read on where AI actually fits in Ghyperion’s stack. And where it does not.

Building the infrastructure beneath global trade is a category. The wedge is the question. This page is one read on where the wedge forms cleanest.

Opening

Ghyperion is early. The landing page is a tagline, the Miami trip was last spring, and the Oppenheimer mentorship and the eMerge trip say the next twelve months are about getting a product into paying hands and closing a first check. That is a specific moment. The decisions made in it compound.

"Infrastructure beneath global trade" is a category, not a wedge. The question is which workflow inside that category is worth building first, and inside that workflow, which parts are genuinely AI problems.

The frame

Most teams building in this space spend too much on LLM calls and not enough on Postgres. The rough split in our experience.

  • 60% Traditional code and database work. User accounts, document storage, filing state machines, payment ledgers.
  • 30% Rule-based logic. Tariff classification rules, customs-filing validation, compliance checks that have to be deterministic and auditable.
  • 10% Genuine AI. Document parsing for messy invoices and bills of lading, HS-code suggestions where the operator still confirms, edge-case reconciliation.

Teams who skip this distinction put an LLM where a SQL query would be faster, cheaper, and defensible in an audit.

Applied to Ghyperion: the right first product is probably a single document-heavy workflow where the customs broker or the SMB exporter is losing hours a week to manual re-entry. AI sits on top of the parsing. Rules sit under the filing. Postgres sits under all of it.

The case study

We ran a founder-level sprint with Andrew Santus at Feeld. Workshop, advisory calls, Organizational Context Architecture, Strategic Operations Framework. The output was not a deck. It was a clear map of which parts of the product needed LLM infrastructure and which parts did not. That mapping is most of the work at a stage where the wrong architecture decision costs a quarter.

The paper

The methodology is published. The Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM), submitted to ACM TiiS. It is the framework we would apply to Ghyperion’s orchestration layer. Repo: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.
